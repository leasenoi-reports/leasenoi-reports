Executive Summary

Strong overall score, but delayed human engagement creates leasing risk.

Our evaluation of Aspendale Scottsdale’s leasing system, benchmarked against 14 Scottsdale multifamily properties, shows a competitive overall score of 71/100. However, the first human response occurred after 10 days, and tour conversion push scored only 6/20. The pattern suggests that demand may be captured initially, but meaningful human engagement and conversion momentum can still break down inside the leasing system.

Delayed Human Response

First human response was 10 days, slower than 83% of responding properties in this benchmark set.

Weak Tour Push

Tour conversion push scored 6/20, indicating limited momentum toward a scheduled tour.

Strong Follow-Up

Follow-up persistence scored 15/15, showing that the system continued to engage after inquiry.

Revenue Visibility

Observed friction may contribute to hidden revenue leakage through slower leasing velocity.
