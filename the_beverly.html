Executive Summary

Severe breakdowns in human engagement created substantial leasing-system risk.

Our evaluation of The Beverly’s leasing system revealed one of the weakest response patterns in the benchmark set. Human engagement did not occur for more than 20 days, significantly exceeding market response expectations. While automated lead capture existed, operational follow-through and conversion momentum appeared materially impaired.

Extremely Delayed Response

First human response occurred after approximately 20.7 days — among the slowest observed in the benchmark set.

Weak Conversion Momentum

Interactions lacked urgency and did not strongly advance toward tour scheduling.

Operational Inconsistency

The leasing system appeared unable to consistently convert initial lead activity into meaningful engagement.

Observed friction may contribute to slower leasing velocity and preventable NOI exposure.
